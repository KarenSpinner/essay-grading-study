ATLAS-Style Argumentative Writing Rubric (Strict-Descriptor Version)
Modeled on the structure of the Arkansas Teaching and Learning Assessment System (ATLAS) ELA writing rubric for grades 6–11. This version uses deliberately demanding score-point descriptors — top scores require exceptional and original work; lower scores apply readily.
Total: 10 points (4 + 4 + 2)

================================================================
DOMAIN 1 — PURPOSE, FOCUS, AND ORGANIZATION (0–4)
================================================================
Score 4 — Reserved for responses that are *exceptional* in focus, structure, and unity. The claim is original, sharply argued, and sustained without a single lapse. The organizational structure is sophisticated and creates not just coherence but real reading momentum, including:
  • an introduction that is striking or insightful, not merely competent,
  • body paragraphs that build on one another (not just list reasons in parallel),
  • transitions that vary and serve the argument, never feeling formulaic,
  • a conclusion that synthesizes rather than restates.
A 4 should be unusual; most strong responses are 3.

Score 3 — The response is competently sustained and adequately focused. The claim is clear and the structure works, but the writing is conventional. Transitions are present but may feel formulaic. The introduction and conclusion do their job without distinguishing themselves. The response does not stumble, but it also does not impress.

Score 2 — The response is loosely sustained. The claim may drift, weaken, or hide behind generality. Organizational structure is present but inconsistent: paragraphs may feel listed rather than developed, transitions may be missing or mechanical, the introduction or conclusion may be perfunctory.

Score 1 — The response is related to the topic but shows weak control of the writing task. The claim is vague or inconsistent. Organization is minimal; ideas appear in a list-like or disconnected way.

Score 0 — Off-topic, unscorable, copied from prompt, or in another language.

================================================================
DOMAIN 2 — EVIDENCE AND ELABORATION (0–4)
================================================================
Score 4 — Reserved for responses with *exceptional and original* support. Evidence is specific, varied, and clearly the result of independent thinking — not the obvious examples a competent student would reach for. Elaboration is substantive and explains *why* each piece of evidence matters in a way that goes beyond restatement. Counterargument is engaged seriously, with a real rebuttal. Word choice is precise and tone is appropriately formal throughout. A 4 should be rare.

Score 3 — Support is adequate. Evidence is mostly relevant and reasonably specific, but predictable — the kinds of examples and sources a careful but unoriginal student would use. Elaboration explains the evidence but does not deepen the argument. Counterargument is acknowledged but treated briefly. Word choice is appropriate but not distinctive.

Score 2 — Support is uneven or cursory. Evidence is vague, generic ("studies show," "doctors say"), or only loosely tied to the claim. Elaboration is thin; the writer may state evidence without explaining its significance. Counterargument is missing or treated dismissively. Word choice is imprecise or shifts in tone.

Score 1 — Support is minimal. Evidence is largely absent, fabricated-sounding, or off-topic. Reasoning is replaced by assertion.

Score 0 — Insufficient or off-topic.

================================================================
DOMAIN 3 — CONVENTIONS OF STANDARD ENGLISH (0–2)
================================================================
Score 2 — Reserved for responses that demonstrate *strong* command of conventions. Errors in grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation are rare, minor, and never interfere with meaning. Sentence structure is varied and consistently correct. A few comma errors or a single typo are tolerable; recurring small errors are not.

Score 1 — The response demonstrates partial command of conventions. There are noticeable errors in grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, or punctuation — even if they do not always interfere with meaning. Comma splices, run-on sentences, missing apostrophes, or recurring punctuation issues drop a response to 1.

Score 0 — Errors are pervasive or interfere with meaning, OR the response is too brief to evaluate.

================================================================
SCORING NOTES
================================================================
• Score each domain independently. A weakness in one domain should not be double-counted in another.
• Use the whole scale. Top scores are reserved for exceptional work; do not award 4s out of generosity.
• If a response is off-topic, refuses the prompt, or is unscorable, assign 0 in all domains.
• Final score = Domain 1 + Domain 2 + Domain 3 (max 10).
